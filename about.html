About


My name is Keith O'Hara. I obtained my Ph.D. in Economics from NYU in 2018, where my research focused on econometrics and computational methods.

I am the author of several open-source C++ libraries and R packages in the areas of:

  • Macroeconometrics
  • Optimal Transport and models of matching
  • Statistics
  • Nonlinear optimization
  • Bayesian simulation methods
  • Compile-time computation of special functions

Other open-source projects that I like and, on occasion, contribute to:

  • The R Project
    • macOS developers: consider joining the dedicated development list.
    • A new group, started by John C. Nash, and supported by the R Consortium, is revisiting many of R's core algorithms. Checkout our progress here.
  • Armadillo C++ linear algebra library
    • An expression template library for matrix-based computation.
    • R wrapper.
